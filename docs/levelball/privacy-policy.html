Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 16, 2026

Caixuan Zeng ("we", "us", or "our") built Level Ball as a mobile game application. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our app. By downloading, installing, or using Level Ball, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the app.

Information We Collect

We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. Level Ball runs entirely on your device and does not require an internet connection. No data leaves your device at any time.

Data Stored on Your Device

Level Ball saves the following data locally on your device to provide a seamless gaming experience:

  • Game progress (unlocked levels)
  • User preferences (sound, haptic feedback, sensitivity, tilt mode, language)

This data is stored only on your device and is never transmitted to us or any third party. You can delete this data at any time by uninstalling the app.

Device Permissions

Level Ball uses your device's motion sensors (accelerometer and gyroscope) to detect tilting, which is the core game mechanic. This sensor data is processed entirely on your device in real time and is never recorded, stored, or transmitted.

Third-Party Services

Level Ball does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, tracking, or data collection services. We do not share any information with third parties.

Children's Privacy

We do not collect personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. Our app is safe for users of all ages and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar regulations.

Your Rights

Since we do not collect any personal data, there is no personal data for us to access, modify, or delete. If you wish to remove all locally stored app data (game progress and preferences), you can simply uninstall the app from your device.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you are entitled to the rights granted under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). If you are a California resident, you are entitled to the rights granted under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). As we do not collect any personal information, these rights are inherently fulfilled.
